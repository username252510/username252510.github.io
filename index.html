Why I Should Get a New Laptop for Back to School

Introduction

This website explains why I think that I should get a new laptop, and not just because I want something new, but because I actually need something reliable that can keep up with school and everyday life. The laptop I use now is reaching its limits. It’s slow, heavy, and doesn’t handle schoolwork very well anymore. I've done my research, and coded this website myself in HTML so please let me know if anything looks off.

Why I Need a New Laptop

I use my laptop for school every day, not just for Google Drive or searching, but also for using more advanced software when needed, which my laptop often struggles to run. Although my current laptop might have been a quick and easy fix when you found out I needed one for school, it is starting to become obsolete.

Reasons for an Upgrade

Windows 10 End of Support

Microsoft is ending support for Windows 10 in October, and my laptop doesn't even meet the minimum system requirements for Windows 11. This means it will no longer receive important security updates and bug fixes, which makes it less safe and less compatible with school software.

Bad Battery

My laptop battery isn't the best, meaning that I often find myself needing to search for outlets just to charge it. I also need to have it plugged in even when it's not even low, because otherwise the battery will be almost dead by the time the class is over. But if I got a new laptop I wouldn't have to worry so much and could charge it when it works for me instead of constantly having it plugged in.

Slow and Underpowered

My current laptop lags when I have too many tabs open, and it struggles when using software like Adobe Illustrator, and one software (Autodesk Fusion) even said that my computer doesn't meet the recommended system requirements, so it doesn't run that well. A more up to date laptop with a more powerful processor and SSD would allow me to use software like this way more easily without the lag.

Heavy and Bulky

My laptop is also pretty heavy, especially when I have to carry it around all day with other materials. Many of the other laptops I looked at are a lot lighter, without giving up any of the important specs. This would make a big difference in my daily life.

Laptops I Researched

Instead of just asking for a random laptop, I looked into different models, compared specs, and thought about what actually matters for how I use it. I’ve focused on portability, reliability, battery life, and value, not just fancy features.

Here’s what I specifically looked for:

  • Portable: easy to carry in my backpack
  • Decent battery: enough to get through school
  • Fast processor and SSD: so I don’t have to wait for things to load
  • Clear and high quality screen: easy on the eyes for reading & writing
  • Build quality: something that is built well
  • Fair price: close to what Spencer’s laptop cost

Responding to Concerns

"Don’t you already have a laptop?"

Yes, and I am grateful for it. But it is starting to become obsolete, and soon won't even get critical software updates. It’s also underpowered for lots of things.

"Is this really a need or just a want?"

It is both. Yes, I do want a better laptop, but I also need something that performs better and fits my day to day life at school.

"So then what do we do with your old laptop?"

I actually have a great idea for that — I could repurpose my current laptop to self-host projects like a Minecraft server for me and my friends. That way it doesn't just sit there unused, and gives me a chance to learn more about tech.
